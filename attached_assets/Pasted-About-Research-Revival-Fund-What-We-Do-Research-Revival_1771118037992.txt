About Research Revival Fund
What We Do
Research Revival Fund is a publishing-led initiative that restores neglected, illegible, or prematurely dismissed research to active circulation. We work with researchers, scholars, and original thinkers to excavate intellectual lineages, clarify claims, and produce canonical artifacts—interviews, dossiers, essays, films—that make forgotten or marginalized inquiry legible again.
Our work addresses a fundamental problem: valuable research disappears not because it lacks merit, but because it lacks the infrastructure, language, or institutional alignment to survive. We provide that infrastructure.
What We Believe
Knowledge is material. Research gets lost through linguistic marginalization, institutional dismissal, unprofitable timing or cultural illegibility. Research needs deliberate preservation. 
Discovery is nonlinear. The way knowledge emerges and re-emerges is oftentimes winding, contingent, influenced by shifting relevance. What seemed marginal in one era may prove essential in another. What appeared illegible within one framework may illuminate another.
Form reveals sensibility. Different artifacts reveal different sensibilities. We publish fluidly to work with the form each research project demands, preserving diversity in ways of knowing.
What We Support
We fund projects unlikely to receive institutional support due to:
Disciplinary illegibility – work that falls between established fields or challenges dominant frameworks
Non-traditional outcomes – projects producing artifacts beyond conventional academic articles
Linguistic or geographic marginalization – research published in overlooked languages or contexts
Collaborative necessity – work requiring teams, translation, or expertise that isolated researchers cannot access alone
Types of Projects, a non-exhaustive list
Recovery and curation of unpublished manuscripts, interviews, or proceedings with expert commentary
Original analysis (essays, visual works, video essays) examining overlooked research teams, labs, or intellectual traditions
Translation and critical editions of historically significant texts in non-dominant languages
Historiographic fictions, documentaries, or multimedia works that make past inquiry accessible to new audiences
Interdisciplinary investigations revealing unexpected sites of innovation across science, technology, culture, and history
Grant size: Up to $10,000
What We Are Not Looking For
Solitary theoretical work without material engagement
Projects replicating what institutions already fund well
How We Work
Research Revival operates on a spectrum of collaboration:
Publishing platform: We work with you to format and disseminate revival work you've already completed, providing editorial guidance and public distribution.
Research support: You're actively working on recovery or analysis but need funding for archival research, translation, materials, or collaborative labor. We provide financial support and optional editorial partnership.
Team assembly: You have an idea but need collaborators. We help build interdisciplinary teams and provide structure for execution.
Our Process
Identification – Through RFPs and active scouting, we surface projects with revival potential
Editorial collaboration – We work with researchers to clarify claims, establish context, and determine optimal artifact forms
Publication – We release work publicly in formats designed for maximum legibility and circulation
Momentum – We provide initial support, then encourage others to build on what we've made accessible
Our Position in the Knowledge Ecosystem
Research Revival Fund has two purposes: to excavate forgotten or marginalized knowledge, and—self-consciously—to rediscover and model alternative approaches to knowledge discovery itself.
We complement institutional scholarship by funding and publishing work that cannot flourish within institutional constraints. We welcome academics and non-academics alike. The only criteria: relentless intellectual obsession and openness to unfamiliar ideas, methodologies, and collaborations.
By making diverse approaches to inquiry tangible and public, Research Revival creates reference points—provocations that expand what seems possible in your own practice, whether scientific research, artistic creation, technological innovation, or humanistic inquiry.
What Makes Us Distinct
We prioritize primary materials and diverse artifact forms. We focus specifically on making what was lost legible again.
Research Revival Fund builds toward an emerging ecosystem of open, experimental publishing where new forms of inquiry can flourish and important ideas can find their way back into circulation.
