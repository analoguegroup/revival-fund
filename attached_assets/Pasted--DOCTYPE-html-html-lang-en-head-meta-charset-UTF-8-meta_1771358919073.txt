        <section>
            <h1>About Research Revival Fund</h1>
            <p class="lead">Research Revival Fund is a publishing-led initiative that restores neglected, illegible, or prematurely dismissed research to active circulation.</p>
            <p>We work with researchers, scholars, and original thinkers to excavate intellectual lineages, clarify claims, and produce canonical artifacts—interviews, dossiers, essays, films—that make forgotten or marginalized inquiry legible again.</p>
            <p>Our work addresses a fundamental problem: valuable research disappears not because it lacks merit, but because it lacks the infrastructure, language, or institutional alignment to survive. We provide that infrastructure.</p>
        </section>

        <section>
            <h2>What We Believe</h2>
            <div class="principles">
                <div class="principle">
                    <h3>Knowledge is material.</h3>
                    <p>Research gets lost through linguistic marginalization, institutional dismissal, unprofitable timing or cultural illegibility. Research needs deliberate preservation.</p>
                </div>
                <div class="principle">
                    <h3>Discovery is nonlinear.</h3>
                    <p>The way knowledge emerges and re-emerges is oftentimes winding, contingent, influenced by shifting relevance. What seemed marginal in one era may prove essential in another. What appeared illegible within one framework may illuminate another.</p>
                </div>
                <div class="principle">
                    <h3>Form reveals sensibility.</h3>
                    <p>Different artifacts reveal different sensibilities. We publish fluidly to work with the form each research project demands, preserving diversity in ways of knowing.</p>
                </div>
            </div>
        </section>

        <section>
            <h2>What We Support</h2>
            <p>We fund projects unlikely to receive institutional support due to:</p>
            
            <div class="two-col">
                <div class="col">
                    <h3>Criteria</h3>
                    <ul>
                        <li><strong>Disciplinary illegibility</strong> – work that falls between established fields or challenges dominant frameworks</li>
                        <li><strong>Non-traditional outcomes</strong> – projects producing artifacts beyond conventional academic articles</li>
                        <li><strong>Linguistic or geographic marginalization</strong> – research published in overlooked languages or contexts</li>
                        <li><strong>Collaborative necessity</strong> – work requiring teams, translation, or expertise that isolated researchers cannot access alone</li>
                    </ul>
                </div>
                <div class="col">
                    <h3>Types of Projects</h3>
                    <ul>
                        <li>Recovery and curation of unpublished manuscripts, interviews, or proceedings with expert commentary</li>
                        <li>Original analysis (essays, visual works, video essays) examining overlooked research teams, labs, or intellectual traditions</li>
                        <li>Translation and critical editions of historically significant texts in non-dominant languages</li>
                        <li>Historiographic fictions, documentaries, or multimedia works that make past inquiry accessible to new audiences</li>
                        <li>Interdisciplinary investigations revealing unexpected sites of innovation across science, technology, culture, and history</li>
                    </ul>
                </div>
            </div>
