    <div class="about-hero">
        <h1>About Research Revival Fund <span class="beta-badge">Beta</span></h1>
        <p class="lead">A publishing-led initiative that restores neglected, illegible, or prematurely dismissed research to active circulation.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="section">
        <span class="section-title">What We Do</span>
        <h2>Infrastructure for Lost Knowledge</h2>
        <p>We work with researchers, scholars, and original thinkers to excavate intellectual lineages, clarify claims, and produce canonical artifacts—interviews, curated dossiers, essays, visual works, and multimedia publications—that make forgotten or marginalized inquiry legible and actionable again.</p>
        
        <p>Our work addresses a fundamental problem: valuable research disappears not because it lacks merit, but because it lacks the infrastructure, language, or institutional alignment to survive. We provide that infrastructure.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="section">
        <span class="section-title">What We Believe</span>
        <h2>First Principles</h2>
        
        <div class="belief-grid">
            <div class="belief-item">
                <h3>Knowledge is materially fragile.</h3>
                <p>Without deliberate preservation and distribution, important ideas vanish—not into the ether, but into obscurity. Research gets lost through linguistic marginalization, institutional dismissal, unprofitable timing, cultural illegibility, or simple administrative chaos. The historical record is full of Peter Putnams and lost notebooks.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="belief-item">
                <h3>Discovery is nonlinear.</h3>
                <p>The way knowledge emerges and re-emerges is winding, contingent, shaped by chance encounters and shifting relevance. What seemed marginal in one era may prove essential in another. What appeared illegible within one framework may illuminate another.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="belief-item">
                <h3>Form shapes circulation.</h3>
                <p>We create artifacts—not just to document research, but to give it new material existence. A well-curated compendium, a rigorous essay, a historiographic fiction: these are technologies for making ideas move again.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>

    <div class="section">
        <span class="section-title">What We Support</span>
        <h2>Criteria for Funding</h2>
        <p>We fund projects unlikely to receive institutional support due to:</p>
        
        <div class="criteria-box">
            <ul class="criteria-list">
                <li><strong>Disciplinary illegibility</strong> – work that falls between established fields or challenges dominant frameworks</li>
                <li><strong>Non-traditional outcomes</strong> – projects producing artifacts beyond conventional academic articles</li>
                <li><strong>Linguistic or geographic marginalization</strong> – research published in overlooked languages or contexts</li>
                <li><strong>Collaborative necessity</strong> – work requiring teams, translation, or expertise that isolated researchers cannot access alone</li>
            </ul>
        </div>

        <div class="project-types">
            <h3>Types of Projects We Fund</h3>
            <ul class="criteria-list">
                <li>Recovery and curation of unpublished manuscripts, interviews, or proceedings with expert commentary</li>
                <li>Original analysis (essays, visual works, video essays) examining overlooked research teams, labs, or intellectual traditions</li>
                <li>Translation and critical editions of historically significant texts in non-dominant languages</li>
                <li>Historiographic fictions, documentaries, or multimedia works that make past inquiry accessible to new audiences</li>
                <li>Interdisciplinary investigations revealing unexpected sites of innovation across science, technology, culture, and history</li>
            </ul>
            <div class="grant-highlight">Grant size: Up to $10,000</div>
        </div>

        <div class="two-col">
            <div></div>
            <div class="negation">
                <h3>What We Decline</h3>
                <ul class="criteria-list">
                    <li>Solitary theoretical work without material engagement</li>
                    <li>Projects replicating what institutions already fund well</li>
                    <li>New scientific research requiring laboratory infrastructure (though we support recovering documentation from past scientific projects)</li>
                </ul>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>

    <div class="section">
        <span class="section-title">How We Work</span>
        <h2>Three Modes of Engagement</h2>
        <p>Research Revival operates on a spectrum of collaboration:</p>
        
        <div class="modes-grid">
            <div class="mode-card">
                <h4>Publishing Platform</h4>
                <p>We work with you to format and disseminate revival work you've already completed, providing editorial guidance and public distribution.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="mode-card">
                <h4>Research Support</h4>
                <p>You're actively working on recovery or analysis but need funding for archival research, translation, materials, or collaborative labor. We provide financial support and optional editorial partnership.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="mode-card">
                <h4>Team Assembly</h4>
                <p>You have an idea but need collaborators. We help build interdisciplinary teams and provide structure for execution.</p>
            </div>
        </div>

        <h3 style="margin-top: 3rem; margin-bottom: 1.5rem; font-size: 1.25rem;">Our Process</h3>
        <div class="process-steps">
            <div class="step">
                <span class="step-number">1</span>
                <span class="step-label">Identification</span>
            </div>
            <div class="step">
                <span class="step-number">2</span>

            </div>
            <div class="step">

            </div>
            <div class="step">
                <span class="step-number">4</span>
                <span class="step-label">Momentum</span>
            </div>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: center; color: var(--shadow); font-size: 0.9375rem; margin-top: 1rem;">Through RFPs and active scouting, we surface projects with revival potential → We work with researchers to clarify claims and determine optimal artifact forms → We release work publicly in formats designed for maximum legibility → We provide initial support, then encourage others to build on what we've made accessible</p>
    </div>

    <div class="section">
        <span class="section-title">Our Position</span>
        <h2>Within the Knowledge Ecosystem</h2>
        <p>Research Revival Fund complements institutional scholarship rather than replacing it. We create space for:</p>
        
        <ul class="criteria-list" style="margin: 2rem 0;">
            <li>Work that cannot reach its full potential within conventional academic structures</li>
            <li>Academics to experiment with forms they cannot pursue through traditional channels (collaborative fictions, multimedia scholarship, cross-disciplinary artifacts)</li>
            <li>Public scholarship that makes primary materials accessible before or alongside traditional academic books</li>
            <li>Interdisciplinary knowledge-building that institutional divisions discourage</li>
        </ul>

        <p>We don't instruct you what to think. We show you other possibilities—other ways of thinking, making, and doing that may directly inspire your own practice, whether in scientific research, fiction, humanistic writing, audiovisual production, or technical innovation.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="section">
        <div class="distinct-box">
            <h2>What Makes Us Distinct</h2>
            <p>Unlike theory journals, we prioritize primary materials and diverse artifact forms. Unlike future-focused publications, we mine the past for present relevance. Unlike discipline-specific presses, we work across boundaries. We share Cabinet's multimedia ambition and Stripe Press's commitment to accessibility, but focus specifically on revival—on making what was lost legible again.</p>
            <p style="margin-top: 1.5rem; margin-bottom: 0;">Research Revival Fund builds toward an emerging ecosystem of open, experimental publishing where new forms of inquiry can flourish and important ideas can find their way back into circulation.</p>
        </div>
