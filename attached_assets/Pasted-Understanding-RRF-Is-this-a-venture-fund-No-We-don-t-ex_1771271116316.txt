Understanding RRF
Is this a venture fund?
No. We don't expect financial returns. But we're excited to see revival projects become launchpads for more ambitious work—including ventures with commercial outcomes.
What counts as "revival"?
Work engaging with research that has been: abandoned due to funding loss, dismissed by paradigm shifts, published in marginalized languages/contexts, buried in archives, or developed within alternative epistemological frameworks that dominant institutions overlooked.
Is This For Me?
Who can apply?
Anyone—academics, independent researchers, artists, scientists, archivists, translators. We care about the quality of the inquiry and depth of engagement, not your institutional affiliation or credentials.
Can I apply from outside the United States?
Yes. Research Revival Fund supports projects globally.
I'm working on my PhD dissertation. Can I apply?
Yes, if your project includes substantial public-facing artifacts we can publish outside the dissertation itself, within the next year.
How do you evaluate "relentless intellectual obsession"?
We look for evidence of sustained engagement—existing preliminary work, depth of knowledge about the research lineage, clarity about why this particular inquiry compels you.
Funding & Timeline
What's the funding range?
Generally $4,000-$10,000, up to $25,000 in special cases.
How long do projects typically last?
We're flexible depending on scope. Once funded, we expect concentrated engagement. Projects are announced when funded and published as they reach milestones or completion. Specify your timeline in the application.
Your Application
Can projects include creative or fictional elements?
Yes—historiographic fictions, speculative documentaries, and other creative forms are welcome if they're grounded in rigorous engagement with the research being revived.
Working Together
What if my project evolves significantly during research?
We expect some evolution. Major scope changes require discussion, but we're flexible if the revival mission remains intact.
Publication & Rights
Do I retain intellectual property rights?
Yes. You retain full IP rights. RRF receives non-exclusive publication and distribution rights to the funded project artifacts, which we determine together during the editorial process.
Where will my work be published?
On the Research Revival Fund platform and potentially through partner publications. All work is publicly accessible.
After Your Project
What happens after publication?
We encourage others to build on the work. You retain the ability to develop it further, and we may feature it in compilations or anthologies.
Can I apply for a second project after completing one?
Yes.
