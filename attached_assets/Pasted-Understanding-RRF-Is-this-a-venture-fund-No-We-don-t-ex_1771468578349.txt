Understanding RRF
Is this a venture fund? No. We don't expect financial returns. But we're excited to see revival projects become launchpads for more ambitious work, including ventures with commercial outcomes. If your project becomes a company, we would be interested in following up with an angel check independently through our network.
What counts as "revival"? Work engaging with research that has been: abandoned due to funding loss, dismissed by paradigm shifts, published in marginalized languages/contexts, buried in archives, or developed within alternative epistemological frameworks that dominant institutions overlooked.
Who Can Apply
Who can apply? Anyone. Academics, independent researchers, artists, scientists, archivists, translators. We care about the quality of the inquiry and depth of engagement, not your institutional affiliation or credentials.
Can I apply from outside the United States? Yes. Research Revival Fund supports projects globally.
I'm working on my PhD dissertation. Can I apply? Yes, if your project includes a substantial public-facing artifact we can publish outside the dissertation itself, within the next year.
How do you evaluate "relentless intellectual obsession"? We look for evidence of sustained engagement: existing preliminary work, depth of knowledge about the research lineage, clarity about why this particular inquiry compels you.
Funding & Timeline
When can I apply? Applications open February 19, 2026. We prioritize reviewing proposals submitted by March 18, 2026. Because we're shaping the fund as we go and want to remain open to exceptional projects, the submission form stays open after this date, but we cannot guarantee review of proposals submitted after March 18.
What's the funding range? Generally $4,000-$10,000, up to $25,000 in special cases.
How long do projects typically last? We're flexible depending on scope. Once funded, we expect concentrated engagement. Projects are announced when funded and published as they reach milestones or completion. Specify your timeline in the application.
Application & Process
How does your selection process work? After the application window closes on March 18, we review submissions with our advisory committee and approve projects on a rolling basis—decisions and announcements happen continuously rather than on a single date.
Can I apply with multiple project ideas? Yes. Submit each project as a separate application so we can evaluate them individually.
Does the research have to be a specific scientific or technical field? No. RRF is an experiment in knowledge inquiry in its own right. We're building our advisory committee and guest reviewers network as we go. Apply with projects from any field, regardless of our current team's backgrounds.
Can projects include creative or fictional elements? Yes. Historiographic fictions, speculative documentaries, and other creative forms are welcome if they're grounded in rigorous engagement with the research being revived.
What if my project evolves significantly during research? We expect some evolution. Major scope changes require discussion, but we're flexible if the revival mission remains intact.
Publication & Beyond
Do I retain intellectual property rights? Yes. You retain full IP rights. RRF receives non-exclusive publication and distribution rights to the funded project artifacts, which we determine together during the editorial process.
Where will my work be published? On the Research Revival Fund platform and potentially through partner publications. All work is publicly accessible.
What happens after publication? We encourage others to build on the work. You retain the ability to develop it further, and we may feature it in compilations or anthologies.
Can I apply for a second project after completing one? Yes.
