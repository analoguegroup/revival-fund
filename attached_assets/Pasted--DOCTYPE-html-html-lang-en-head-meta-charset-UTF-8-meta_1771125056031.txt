            <h1>About Research Revival Fund</h1>
        </div>

        <div class="section">
            <span class="section-label">What We Do</span>
            <p>Research Revival Fund is a publishing-led initiative that restores neglected, illegible, or prematurely dismissed research to active circulation. We work with researchers, scholars, and original thinkers to excavate intellectual lineages, clarify claims, and produce canonical artifacts—interviews, dossiers, essays, films—that make forgotten or marginalized inquiry legible again.</p>
            <p>Our work addresses a fundamental problem: valuable research disappears not because it lacks merit, but because it lacks the infrastructure, language, or institutional alignment to survive. We provide that infrastructure.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="section">
            <span class="section-label">What We Believe</span>
            
            <div class="card">
                <h3>Knowledge is material.</h3>
                <p>Research gets lost through linguistic marginalization, institutional dismissal, unprofitable timing or cultural illegibility. Research needs deliberate preservation.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="card">
                <h3>Discovery is nonlinear.</h3>
                <p>The way knowledge emerges and re-emerges is oftentimes winding, contingent, influenced by shifting relevance. What seemed marginal in one era may prove essential in another. What appeared illegible within one framework may illuminate another.</p>
            </div>
            
            <div class="card">
                <h3>Form reveals sensibility.</h3>
                <p>Different artifacts reveal different sensibilities. We publish fluidly to work with the form each research project demands, preserving diversity in ways of knowing.</p>
            </div>
        </div>

        <div class="section">
            <span class="section-label">What We Support</span>
            <h2>Criteria for Funding</h2>
            <p>We fund projects unlikely to receive institutional support due to:</p>
            
            <ul class="item-list">
                <li><strong>Disciplinary illegibility</strong> – work that falls between established fields or challenges dominant frameworks</li>
                <li><strong>Non-traditional outcomes</strong> – projects producing artifacts beyond conventional academic articles</li>
                <li><strong>Linguistic or geographic marginalization</strong> – research published in overlooked languages or contexts</li>
                <li><strong>Collaborative necessity</strong> – work requiring teams, translation, or expertise that isolated researchers cannot access alone</li>
            </ul>

            <h2 style="margin-top: 3rem; font-size: 1.5rem;">Types of Projects, a non-exhaustive list</h2>
            <ul class="item-list">
                <li>Recovery and curation of unpublished manuscripts, interviews, or proceedings with expert commentary</li>
                <li>Original analysis (essays, visual works, video essays) examining overlooked research teams, labs, or intellectual traditions</li>
                <li>Translation and critical editions of historically significant texts in non-dominant languages</li>
                <li>Historiographic fictions, documentaries, or multimedia works that make past inquiry accessible to new audiences</li>
                <li>Interdisciplinary investigations revealing unexpected sites of innovation across science, technology, culture, and history</li>
            </ul>
            
            <div class="grant-badge">Grant size: Up to $10,000</div>

            <div class="negation">
                <h3>What We Are Not Looking For</h3>
                <ul class="item-list" style="margin-top: 0;">
                    <li>Solitary theoretical work without material engagement</li>
                    <li>Projects replicating what institutions already fund well</li>
                </ul>
            </div>
        </div>

        <div class="section">
            <span class="section-label">How We Work</span>
            <p>Research Revival operates on a spectrum of collaboration:</p>
            
            <div class="grid-3">
                <div class="card">
                    <h3>Publishing platform</h3>
                    <p>We work with you to format and disseminate revival work you've already completed, providing editorial guidance and public distribution.</p>
                </div>
                <div class="card">
                    <h3>Research support</h3>
                    <p>You're actively working on recovery or analysis but need funding for archival research, translation, materials, or collaborative labor. We provide financial support and optional editorial partnership.</p>
                </div>
                <div class="card">
                    <h3>Team assembly</h3>
                    <p>You have an idea but need collaborators. We help build interdisciplinary teams and provide structure for execution.</p>
                </div>
            </div>

            <div class="process">
                <div class="process-step">

            <p style="text-align: center; color: var(--shadow); font-size: 0.9375rem; margin-top: 2rem;">Through RFPs and active scouting, we surface projects with revival potential → We work with researchers to clarify claims, establish context, and determine optimal artifact forms → We release work publicly in formats designed for maximum legibility and circulation → We provide initial support, then encourage others to build on what we've made accessible</p>
        </div>

        <div class="section">
            <span class="section-label">Our Position in the Knowledge Ecosystem</span>
            <p>Research Revival Fund has two purposes: to excavate forgotten or marginalized knowledge, and—self-consciously—to rediscover and model alternative approaches to knowledge discovery itself.</p>
            
            <p>We complement institutional scholarship by funding and publishing work that cannot flourish within institutional constraints. We welcome academics and non-academics alike. The only criteria: relentless intellectual obsession and openness to unfamiliar ideas, methodologies, and collaborations.</p>
            
            <p>By making diverse approaches to inquiry tangible and public, Research Revival creates reference points—provocations that expand what seems possible in your own practice, whether scientific research, artistic creation, technological innovation, or humanistic inquiry.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="highlight-box">
            <h2>What Makes Us Distinct</h2>
            <p>We prioritize primary materials and diverse artifact forms. We focus specifically on making what was lost legible again.</p>
            <p>Research Revival Fund builds toward an emerging ecosystem of open, experimental publishing where new forms of inquiry can flourish and important ideas can find their way back into circulation.</p>
